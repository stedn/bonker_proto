Origin

This project originated from a silly grad student Art+Science project at the University of Chicago about 5 years ago. Basically, they said they'd give $100 to the first and second prize winners who delivered original works of art that had some underpinning in science. There were two categories: one in video and one in music. I entered both.

To start, I haphazardly produced a tremendously stupid video of dropping bits of rice onto a piece of paper. Afterwards, for the music category, I spent a proper week of work devising a genius method for generating jazz improvization from a statistical mechanical Ising model. The stupid rice video won first prize in the video category while the tunable, automated jazz generator only one second prize in music. What can I say?

I'll admit that the melody I picked wasn't very great and the synthesizer was terrible, but it was only a proof of concept. I still feel like the idea was quite astonishing coming from a farm-boy in his first year of graduate school, and I can't understand how these fantastic physicists and chemists at the university couldn't understand it. (Well, now that I've worked with them for a few more years I understand better).

I wrote the code for the original composition in MATLAB, and then converted the output to sound with Csound. It wasn't done elegantly, and but fortunately, I happened to keep a hold of it.

I won't go into the original code that much because I built a thoroughly better version, relying on my much-imporved understanding of statistical techniques. I now understand that essentially what i had dreamed up was just a rudimentary form of Boltzmann machine—a precursor to the neural net.
