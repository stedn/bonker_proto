We are living in the most frivolous and ironic century, but we can change that. Join in a fight for sincerity. You deserve a better "About me" page.

Because isn't reason enough anymore.

Below, I've summarized my views on what makes up my self, my belief in a purpose of existence, and how I decide how I should act. I hope you can appreciate this effort, and that you consider the reasons that motivate you too.

My identity is composite and continuous

When I say "me" I'm not talking about an atomic individual or a subjective internal mind. When I say "me," I mean a continuum of streams of information.

I must believe the universe has a purpose

I have derived a thought experiment that, taken with a few reasonable premises, has led me to the conclusion that the universe has a purpose.

The proper course of action must come from intuition

Importantly, just because I accept that the universe has a purpose, this doesn't mean that I know this purpose. Without knowing the purpose, I cannot derive a clear course of action to achieve the purpose. Using another thought experiment, I've concluded that the only method of ascertaining ethical action must follow from revealed intuition.

I optimize over a multitude of intuitions

My intuitions are composite, impermanent, and hard to pin down, and yet they are all that's left to tell me what to do. I can never clearly know what is best to do so I can only choose my actions from an inmeasurable probabilistic function that I will never be able to know with certainty.

So this is what I do.

What do you do?
